Decisions time

Maikel Wednesday, 6 April 2022 diary

This post is going to run for a while. I've invested the last couple of weeks in quitting caffeine. I've been caffeine free for 2 weeks, right now I'm heading back to it.

Why am I going back to coffee?

Dullness. Life becomes meaningless without it. It's not an state of depression after the 3rd day but a state of carelessness. It is literally the opposite from caring too much about everything to not caring at all. What I want is balance and to get balance I'm going to need coffee. So what I need to mind about is the tolerance to it.

I'm going to drink just enough to have a meaningful workout and sleep whenever needed. So if that coffee makes me sleepy, I'm sleeping. Same as my calories are distributed throughout the day I'm going to let the sleeping hours do the same.

Rethinking the gym routine.

I haven't gone to the gym either, the lack of care for life of quitting coffee had made going to the gym a shore. Which is another of the reasons I'm going back or specifically going back to coffee pre-workout only.

I've tried to no avail for 2 weeks to rotate my sleeping pattern to wake up at 4:30am and I simply can't without the aid of coffee. Even the sleeping pills (Dormidina) only help me sleep a couple of hours during the day when I try to sleep early. So I haven't gone at all to the gym.

I'm quit maintenance now and head for heavy fat loss.

UPDATE: So I've gone to the gym after having 1 coffee and it cost me nothing, it's really MUCH more pleasurable to go caffeinated than decaf. I also remade my workout routine (pec/back everyday except when legs in a configuration of day 1, day legs, day 2, day legs) so I train every major muscle (pec & back) more often.

This blog

I should stick to one post until that post is completed. If there are multiple subjects within that post I should chunk them only when writing in that post is over once it becomes too big.

Where am I living?

This is one of the most annoying subjects because it is heavily interconnected to other subjects. Facts:

  • Where I live might dictate where I can work, unless I count remote. That is, I could find a remote work and stay wherever I actually want.
  • The sensible thing would be to stay nearby for as long as the armpit surgery is done.

Health stuff and location

Teeth

This week's 8th of April (Friday) I'm getting, in theory, the aligners put back on. That means freedom to move around for 3 months from that date. So in theory until July I can be wherever. In reality this become a non-issue if I simply get a job within Cadiz province. In an ideal scenario I'd like to stay in Spain until my teeth treatment is fully done.

Armpits

I'm in waiting list. In theory I'm there for up to six months. The doctor said they usually perform it within 3 months. But online news says on average Puerta del Mar (the hospital) has a 160 days waiting list. That means it can take until October for me to get the surgery.

Technically I could move wherever as far as it's somewhere that allows me to come back. The problem is not what I do while I'm out. The problem is once I get the surgery I might not be able to do anything (I'd be temporarily incapacitated) for a few weeks.

Location

So where am I moving in May is the main question that this post intends to answer. The price of anything for May in AirBnB negates not working, meaning it is entirely unaffordable without a job. If I stay in Cádiz I would need to pull out a favour. What I would like:

  1. A good gym where I can train without it being at ridiculous AM.
  2. Green areas to walk around, pine forest or just forests.

So this table is the 3 options that come to mind right now but I'm not limited to them.
