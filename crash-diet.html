Crash diet

Maikel Saturday, 22 January 2022 diary

I've been feeling bloated this week. I was on a bulking cycle and that's normal yet to a point. I started to dislike my image on the mirror, especially around my neck which was getting chubby again very quickly.

So I started this crash diet on the middle of Wednesday the 19th after Luismi left. The idea was pretty basic:

  • 20-minute HIIT in intervals of 1-minute at 5 kmph then 1-minute at 11 kmph.
  • Weightlifting on series of 3 per exercise as usual when cutting.
  • Intermittent "broken" DRY fasting from around 5 or 6pm onwards 16 hours minimum.
  • LISS everyday. Just getting out and waking around for an hour or longer.
  • 1 pill of Synephrine with 1 vial of L-Carnitine immediately before heading to the gym.
  • Measure caloric consumption with MyFitnessPal.
  • Break the dry part of the fasting after the gym with just 1 coffee with no milk, so you're still fasting because coffee has no calories.

The results:

I'm ignoring body fat and muscle mass as both are wildly inconsistent, instead let's focus on accurate targets:

My weight has lowered by quite a lot and I feel far less bloated. I didn't stop having protein nor creatine.

Things I noticed:

  1. The first day this is incredibly easy to do. The second day is easy. The third day was when it started to get hard.
  2. As usual during a fast I get over energised by it.
  3. For first time in a long time I can run and not any run. I could run at a pace of 11 kms per hour which is not the leisure pace I'm accustomed to.

  1. The fact my gym treadmill has the game solitaire makes the running time fly effortlessly. While at 5 I play normal, while at 11 I just look at all possible movements. It's exactly as above.
  2. The weightlifting in a fasted state does not bother my performance in the slightest.
  3. On the third day it becomes impossible to continue. That was the day I dropped HIIT, I simply couldn't do it.
  4. I could feel on the third day I couldn't go any heavier nor train at my strongest, unlike the previous too.
  5. It could be caused by having had the fast started wayyy too early this third day at around 2pm instead of 6pm. I should have kept it consistent. But I woke up early (I wake up usually past noon) because I had a package of protein delivered and the driver woke me up when he pressed the doorbell.
  6. I still feel, even considering the previous one, that I hit the max low I can go doing this.
  7. The novelty of this crash diet is I got around 100 grams or more of protein every day during my feeding period. Other times I've done it I just ate anything in low amounts without paying much attention to macros.
  8. I've eaten roughly 1200 calories only per day during this period.
  9. All I'm losing is obviously water and if I were back on a bulk I would just regain it just as quick. I need to find a middle path.
  10. The third day unsurprisingly, only 100 grams were lost in the gym. When I do HIIT I literally lose a kilogram. I know it's just water but water matters.

During the day I took several measurements, the top of each curve is always just before I start each fasting period, so at my heaviest. The bottom is the official weight, the consistent one I get when I wake up. Therefore I started 3 days ago with 73.1kgs and tomorrow morning predictably I'll be around 3 kgs lighter.

Now the other accurate measure is waist circumference. That is now FILL THIS UP.

Some conclusions:

  • I need to prevent water retention.
  • One of the ways I can control water consumption is by looking at my weight. Chosing a figure I can't go above despite cycling bulking/cutting.
  • That figure could be 70 kgs because anything above that and I start looking bloated.
  • Limiting the amount of protein shakes taken per day can also help with water retention. I can do this by getting more daily doses protein from meat and fish instead of liquid forms.
  • Strangely the more water I drink, the less I seem to flush out.
  • Consuming creatine or not makes no difference to water retention. While not consuming it, shortly starts to affect my performance when weightlifting.
  • This diet is not feasible for longer periods than 3 days.
