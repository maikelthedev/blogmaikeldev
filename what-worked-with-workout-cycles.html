What worked with workout cycles.

Maikel Friday, 15 October 2021 dieting

The Goal

Reaching 78kgs with 13% body fat and less than 80 cms of waist line. The current priority is body fat.

Purpose

The purpose of doing these cycles is to build up as much muscle as possible to burn as much fat as possible afterwards. No muscle, no fat burning. The reason I switched to them is that body recomposition stopped working. That means, trying to get lean while gaining weight seems impossible to me. Gaining muscle (especially pecs) when I moved to cycling phases improved visibly.

The EMS is used every weekday in the abdominal area The EMS is not used because I'm already fit enough to need it so I'm keeping the EMS for only pain avoidance with its TENS function

Basic Parameters

You bulk up until you reach 73 kgs. or more, then note down the results (see below) and go down on the cutting cycle until you reach 68 kgs. or less. Rinse and repeat until you're below 15% body fat. Once there maintain a threshold of 10 to 15% independently on weight as you'll start to weight more and more, so there would be a shift like 70 to 75 window and more.

The basic changes of today

Today (15th of October) I start a cutting cycle. After I started a bulking one on the 16th of September (2021). That means I've been bulking up for a month.

I've written down the results in this private spreadsheet here to be able to trace basically:

  • Body fat when started each cycle
  • Exact weight.
  • Skeletal muscle kilograms.

I'm keeping the same dieting habit of 242 grams of protein per day. I'm typing here all parameters I've discovered so far that I had handwritten in my notepad.

The cycles

Common stuff to both

Diet

  • Eat 242 grams of proteins per day.
  • Each meal must be high in protein instead of too much protein in one meal. This also helps with satiety.
  • Track your macros with MyFitnessPal.
  • Add fibre to every meal.
  • No more than 1 scoop of protein at a time in a shake or it'll give you diarrhoea. Add psyllium husks to each shake.
  • Eat as lean as possible.
  • Meal planning to avoid spending the day counting calories.

Workout

  • Perform 4 to 5 exercises of 4 x 8 to 4 x 10 reps when bulking.
  • Perform the same exercises at 3 x 8 to 3 x 10 when cutting.
  • Always use Workit to track your work outs.
  • 90 second break between series.
  • Avoid overtraining (4 or 5 exercises per day seems enough) to not get bored of the gym.

Bulking up rules

Diet

  • Eat 242 grams of protein per day.
  • Set up MyFitnessPal to measure macros, with a 0.5 kg extra per week (3100 calories).
  • Eat highly caloric lean food (cereals, lean red meat).
  • Help yourself with carbs using maltodextrin on every shake.
  • Eat every 3 hours. Change names of meals in MFP accordingly.

Workout

  • No cardio allowed, just walking.

Cutting rules

This cycle might be shorter than bulking up.

Diet

  • Do intermittent fasting as described here Intermittent fasting (maikel.uk)
  • Set up MyFitnessPal to measure macros, with a 0.5 kg loss per week (2100 1900 calories).
  • Eat fruits for fibre and low macros, low calories (fruits, green veg) to achieve satiety faster.
  • Use the AIP foods as a list of what to eat since more are low density.
  • Intermittent wet fasting 16 hours per day. (see: https://www.maikel.uk/intermittent-fasting-is-cancelled/)
  • TRY: Don't eat at night so IF from 2pm to 6am. (see: https://www.maikel.uk/intermittent-fasting-is-cancelled/)

Workout

  • Add cardio, any cardio.  Find the one that works. LISS seems to work better.
  • If you don't feel like running, walk 5K. Even walking burns fat.
  • Reach 15k steps minimum. Walk whatever is needed to reach your weight goal for that day, not more.

Extra discovery:

  • After just 1 week between the last press  bench exercise to the next something happened. I was able to lift 45 kgs in ten reps  for 4 series. The next week (one week between workouts as I had a non-planned deload because of deadlift causing a lower back sprain), I struggled to lift 50 kgs, the next weight. This suggests supercompensation benefits disappear after 1 week. If I had trained 48 hours later I could have lifted more.
  • By doing legs only once a week I'm wasting precious resources that could be burning more fat, since my legs are my strongest muscles. So I'm going to try adding Saturdays as the second leg day.
