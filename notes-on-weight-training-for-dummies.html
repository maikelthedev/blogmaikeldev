Notes on Weight Training For Dummies

Maikel Sunday, 17 October 2021 readingsummaries

I read this book to have a quick review of my own knowledge. It wasn't enough thought, it's the second one I read on the subject, being the first one the DeLavier one, to remember all the possible exercises.

The notes

  • "When you lose muscle your metabolism slows" I highlighted this one as a reminder of why am I bulking up, to speed up fat loss.
  • "Gauging your fitness level" but the book didn't really tell me a way to actually do this. Like a list of exercises that could tell me my exact strenght.
  • "Perform exercises through the fullest range of motion" a reminder.
  • "To build the largest, strongest muscles that your genetic makeup allows, perform relatively few repetitions".
  • Low reps = higher risk of injury. High reps = lower risk of injury.
  • Routines should include exercise for each of the following group of muscles: glutes, quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, chest, back, abs, delts, biceps, triceps. All other muscles are optional.
  • "Follow the rule of training from larger muscle to smaller".
  • "Don't exercise the same muscle two days in a row" I put this one in bold as it is the one I am more concerned about as I'm getting results doing exactly the opposite of this. "If you ignore the 48-hour rule weight lifting might make you weaker rather than stronger". Well that's not my experience, I keep lifting more. I might reconsider this one if I ever plateau.
  • "You need to hit each muscle group at least twice a week", good I do that, except with legs.
  • "Triceps and chest" should not be exercised on consecutive days.
  • Superseries save you time by reducing the amount of rest you need between series, especially when they are two different muscles.
  • "Benefit of HIIT is losing the highest amount of fat".
  • "HIIT , 30 seconds job, 30 seconds max run".
  • "Wear training gloves when using bands for maximum comfort".

So as you see there's not much to learn. Doubts on the not, same day rule, I identified I shouldn't care about much of this until I plateau. But the book was rather bullshit to be fair.
